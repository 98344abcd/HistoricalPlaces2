History Of India
  • India's history is marked by the rise and fall of many empires, the spread of religion and culture, and the development of cities. Early history Humans arrive Modern humans arrived in India between 73,000 and 55,000 years ago. Indus Valley Civilization The Indus Valley Civilization began around 3000 BCE in the western Indus river basin. Vedic Age The Vedic Age was a period of great importance in Indian history, and the source of Hindu religion, philosophy, and social customs. Jainism and Buddhism These religions emerged in response to the caste system that became predominant in Hinduism.
  • Ancient History Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2500–1900 BCE): One of the world's oldest urban civilizations, located in present-day Pakistan and northwestern India. Known for advanced town planning, drainage systems, and trade. Vedic Period (c. 1500–500 BCE): Marked by the composition of the Vedas, sacred texts that laid the foundation for Hinduism. The period saw the rise of various kingdoms and the social stratification known as the caste system. Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE): Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, this empire unified most of India. Ashoka, its most notable ruler, embraced Buddhism and spread its teachings across Asia.
  • Classical Period Gupta Empire (c. 320–550 CE): Known as the Golden Age of India, marked by significant achievements in science, mathematics, art, and literature. The period saw the development of the number system and astronomical advancements. Regional Kingdoms: After the Gupta Empire, various regional kingdoms emerged, such as the Chalukyas, Chola, and Pallava in South India, contributing to the rich cultural tapestry. Medieval Period Islamic Invasions (c. 700–1200 CE): Various invasions by Islamic rulers, including the Turks and later the Mughals, led to significant cultural and religious changes in India. Mughal Empire (1526–1857): Founded by Babur, this empire is known for its cultural achievements and architectural marvels, like the Taj Mahal. Akbar the Great, one of its greatest emperors, promoted liberal policies and religious tolerance. Colonial Era British Colonization (1858–1947): After the decline of the Mughal Empire, the British East India Company gained control, leading to widespread changes in administration, economy, and infrastructure. The rebellion of 1857 marked the beginning of significant nationalist movements. Indian Independence Movement: Key figures like Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, and Subhas Chandra Bose emerged, advocating for independence through non-violent resistance and political activism. Modern India Independence (1947): India gained independence from British rule on August 15, 1947, leading to the partition of British India into India and Pakistan. This was marked by widespread communal violence and mass migrations. Post-Independence Era: India adopted a democratic framework, and Nehru became its first Prime Minister. The country has since pursued economic reforms, technological advancements, and a significant role in global politics.

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century CE Hindu Sun temple at Konark about 35 kilometres (22 mi) northeast from Puri city on the coastline in Puri district, Odisha, India. The temple is attributed to king Narasingha Deva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty about 1250 CE.

In the heart of Ayodhya, a city that echoes the ancient tales of the Ramayana, stands the magnificent Ayodhya Ram Temple. The Ayodhya Ram Mandir stands as a symbol deeply rooted in the rich faith and culture. As you embark on a journey through the history and significance of this Ayodhya heritage site, you’ll discover its profound impact on India’s spiritual and cultural landscape.

The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers. Hawa Mahal is known as the “palace of winds“. Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh built it palace in 1799. Hawa Mahal is considered to be unique as it has many small windows and balconies that seem like a honeycomb.
